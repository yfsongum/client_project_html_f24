International Center

At the University of Michigan International Center (IC), we're here to support international students in every step of their journey at the University of Michigan. Whether you're new to campus or looking for ongoing support, we offer a wide range of services, resources, and opportunities that are tailored just for you.

Immigration and Visa

We are committed to helping international students navigate the immigration and visa-related issues, including:

  • F-1 and J-1 visas: We provide guidance for the initial application and maintaining status of your student visas.
  • Work Authorization: We guide you through the process of applying for OPT (Optional Practical Training) or CPT (Curricular Practical Training) authorizations so you can gain valuable work experience in the U.S.
  • Travel Support: Unsure about travel restrictions or documentation requirements? We help ensure your travels are smooth and stress-free.

Cultural Challanges and Support

Adjusting to life in a new country can be overwhelming, but you don't have to face it alone. Reaching out to us! We offer:

  • International Student Orientation: A fun and informative way to get familiar with campus life and meet other students.
  • Cross-cultural Programs: Events and activities to help you meet students all over the world and explore new cultures.
  • Counseling and Wellness Resources: We partner with campus health services (i.e., CAPS) to help connect with you to counselors who understand the unique challenges international students face.

Academic and Career Guidance

Stressed about academic success in the U.S.? Here are several opportunities for you to learn about the U.S. academic system:

  • Academic Workshops: Workshops focused on study strategies, classroom expectations, and so forth.
  • Career Development: From resume building to interview prep, we'll help you navigate career fairs and job searches, both in the U.S. and abroad.
  • Academic Advising: You can always seek for help from advisors regarding academic, personal, and immigration-related questions.

Scholarships and Funding

Finances can be a big concern when living and studying in a foreign country. You may check the following scholorships and funding options:

  • Financial Aid Opportunities
  • Emergency Funds

Leadership and Community Building

Looking to get involved and make a difference? Join International Student Advisory Committee (ISAC) and have a voice in shaping programs and policies for international students at UM!

Resources for Dependents and Families

If you're here with a spouse or family, we hold info sessions to provide guidance for school enrollment, childcare, and healthcare services for your family.
